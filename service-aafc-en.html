Career Focus Program

From Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada

How to get started

How to get started

Career Focus provides funding to organizations for the creation of agricultural internships that provide career-related work experiences for recent graduates.

Each year, employers must apply between December 1 and December 30 for their project to be considered. If approved, projects can start on or after April first of the following year. Approved projects will be eligible to receive 50% of total eligible costs, up to a maximum of $20,000 in matching funds. Applications will be reviewed on a first come, first served basis as long as funds are available.

Applicants are strongly encouraged to apply early. 

Who is eligible for this service

Eligible employers

Eligible employers are organizations who can offer projects that will give employees:

  • agriculture career-related work experiences in Canada; and
  • skills acquisition through mentoring and coaching.

Note: eligible applicants must be legal entities capable of entering into legally binding agreements.

Eligible projects

Projects must offer the employee meaningful work (internship) that may last up to one year and be for a minimum of four months. The project must last long enough to give the employee significant experience and possibly lead to long-term employment or self-employment.

Recruitment of participants/Interns

Contribution recipients are responsible for recruiting participants for Career Focus activities and verifying the eligibility of all youth who are recruited. To qualify, participants must be:

  • between the ages of 15 and 30;
  • unemployed or under-employed;
  • have graduated in the last three calendar years from:
    • an agriculture-related program in a university, college or CEGEP; or
    • a provincial program focusing on biological, agricultural and veterinary science or applied technology; and
  • Canadian citizen or permanent resident.

What happens after you apply

We may contact you for additional information. Once you submit an application we will:

  1. send an acknowledgement email to you within 1 business day;
  2. review and assess your application against the parameters and criteria of the program; and
  3. notify you in writing of final approval or rejection within 100 business days.

There are currently no delays in processing applications.

Our current application processing time is less than 100 business days.

How to apply

A complete online application package consists of a completed Project Application Form and the following attachments:

  • Signature of Applicant Authorized Representative (required)
  • Consent to Disclose Personal and/or Business Information Form (required)
  • Official Language Minority Community Assessment (voluntary)

Applicants in the Province of Québec may be required to complete an additional form to comply with the Act Respecting the Ministère du Conseil Exécutif (M-30). For more information, contact the Quebec regional office.

All projects must comply with the Canadian Environmental Assessment Act, 2012 (CEAA 2012). For more information regarding this requirement, refer to the Program Guide.

More information about this service

More information about this service

Available funding

The Career Focus Program within Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada will provide $864,000 each year to projects that hire recent graduates in agriculture, agri-food science and veterinary medicine.

Eligible costs

Career Focus Program funds may only be allocated for salaries and social benefits. The employer is responsible for paying the employee.

Ineligible costs or limitations

The Career Focus Program only supports salaries and benefits for qualified interns.

Because of the demand for internships, applicants will be limited to one application per round until all eligible applications have been assessed. If funds exist to support more than one application per applicant, additional applications will be considered.

If your project is approved

Successful Applicants who enter into a Contribution Agreement (CA) with AAFC will be referred to therein as Recipients. Recipients are required to enter into a CA that outlines the amount of funding AAFC will provide to the Recipient toward eligible costs, as well as the Recipient's responsibilities and obligations.

Payments to Recipients

AAFC will make payments based on claims submitted for eligible costs incurred and paid by the Recipient in accordance with the terms and conditions of the CA. Each claim must be supported by details of the costs being claimed and certified by an authorized official of the Recipient.

For projects of less than eight months, AAFC will issue 100% of funds at the end of the internship, upon receipt of the final report, proof of salary payment to the candidate, and Financial Engagement form.

For projects of eight to 12 months AAFC will issue 50% of funds after the first half of the internship, only upon receipt of the Financial Engagement form and proof of salary payment to the candidate. The payment of the last 50% is made at the end of the internship, upon receipt of the final report, the Financial Commitment form and proof of salary payment to the candidate.

Career Focus Program funds may only be allocated for salaries and social benefits. The employer is responsible for paying the employee.

Reporting requirements

The Employer is responsible for completing and submitting to Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada the required documents and evaluation reports at the mid-point and/or at the end of the Project and to participate in program evaluation which could occur after 12 months and 36 months.

Evaluation and participant information forms

  • Participant Information Form
  • Final Report of the Employer Form
  • Employee's Assessment Report on the Project Form
  • Financial Claim Form
